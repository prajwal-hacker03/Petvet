Frequently Asked Questions

Get quick answers to the most common questions faced by people when encountering a street pet.

I found a street pet. What are the first three things I should do?

First, secure the pet safely without getting bitten or scratched; if they are aggressive or scared, use a blanket or lure them with food. Second, immediately check for severe wounds and offer clean water (never force them to drink). Third, check for ID tags, microchips, and keep them in a warm, dry, isolated space away from other household animals until you consult a veterinarian.

Can I give cow's milk to a rescued puppy or kitten?

Absolutely NOT! Cow's milk contains high lactose levels that cannot be digested by puppies and kittens, leading to severe dehydration, vomiting, and diarrhea, which can be fatal. Instead, use a commercial Puppy or Kitten Milk Replacer (KMR). In emergencies, use plain warm water mixed with a tiny pinch of salt and honey/glucose to rehydrate them until you obtain formula.

How does the Adoption Hub work? Is it free?

Yes, our Adoption Hub is 100% free! It is designed to connect kind rescuers who are fostering street animals with prospective loving families. You can upload pet details, specify rescue location, list vaccines administered, and add contact details. We vet listings in the session to protect animals from malicious exploiters.

Phase 1

First 24 Hours: Emergency Rescue Protocol

Finding a stray on the street can be chaotic. Here is what to do to stabilize the pet safely and correctly.

1

Assess & Secure Safely

Keep calm. Assess the animal's behavior. If injured, use a thick blanket or towel to gently cover their eyes to calm them down, and lift them slowly without twisting their limbs.

2

Stabilize & Check Wounds

Place them in an isolated, well-ventilated carrier or quiet room. Inspect gently for deep cuts, bleeding, ticks, or broken bones. Do not apply harsh chemical disinfectants.

3

Hydrate (No Force Feeding)

Offer lukewarm water immediately. Dehydration is the #1 killer. If weak, place droplets of electrolyte water on their lips. Never force liquids down their throat as it causes aspiration.

Critical First Aid Checklist

  • Maintain body heat: Place them on a warm towel or hot water bottle wrapped in cloth.
  • Isolate from existing household pets to prevent viral/parasitic transmission.
  • Do not vaccinate or administer tick spot-on treatments immediately if weak or malnourished.

Food & Nutrition

Warm puppy milk replacer (KMR/esbilac) fed via syringe/dropper every 2 hours. Absolutely no solid foods or cow milk.

Key Care Tips

Help them urinate/defecate by rubbing their belly and groin gently with a warm, damp cotton ball after each meal.

Critical Precautions: Keep them constantly warm. Hypothermia sets in rapidly. Do not let them sleep directly on bare floors.

Feeding Chart & Toxic Foods

Rescued street pets have highly sensitive stomachs, often worsened by starvation. Ensure you only feed safe ingredients.

Safe Ingredients

  • Boiled boneless chicken (no salt/spices)
  • Cooked white rice or pumpkin mash
  • Plain scrambled eggs (no butter)

Toxic & Dangerous

  • Chocolate & Cocoa products (fatal)
  • Onions, garlic, and chives
  • Grapes, raisins, and cooked bones

Frequency

3 to 4 times daily for growing pups/kittens; twice daily for stable adult animals.

Water Intake

Fresh water must be accessible 24/7. Rescued animals typically require 50-70ml per kg daily.

Grooming, Cleaning & Parasite Control

Stray animals often harbor external parasites, dirt, or dermatological issues. Follow these steps to clean them safely.

Flea & Tick Management

Never bathe a severely weak animal. Brush them gently. Use a comb dipped in soapy water to manually strip fleas. Consult a vet before applying chemical spot-ons.

First Bath Protocol

Only bathe once the pet has eaten and stabilized for 3-4 days. Use lukewarm water and gentle baby/oatmeal shampoo. Blow dry immediately so they do not catch a cold.

Sanitizing the Space

Use pet-safe diluted vinegar or enzymatic cleaners to scrub their room. Avoid bleach, which irritates their respiratory tracts.

Activity, Sleep & Health Indicators

Establish healthy sleep structures and understand behavioral guidelines for a healthy transition.

Sleep Requirements

Puppies and kittens sleep up to 18-20 hours. Ensure their den is in a low-traffic zone where they feel entirely secure from startles.

Exercise & Play

Limit outdoor walks initially to prevent exposure to diseases like Parvovirus. Focus on indoor stimulation and gentle bonding toys.

Common Disease Warnings

Monitor for: lethargy, watery nose/eyes, loose stools, or lack of appetite. These are signs of viral infections requiring vet attention.

Emergency Rescue Toolkit

First-hand advice on handling injured, weak, or dehydrated street pets. Keep yourself safe while saving a life.

Common Rescue Scenarios

Click on any scenario below to view instant, step-by-step first aid guides.

Injury

1. How to Handle Injured Street Animals

Injured animals are highly terrified and prone to biting out of self-defense, even if they are naturally friendly.

  • Safety First: Put on thick gloves. Keep your face away from their head. If it's a dog, you can make a temporary muzzle using soft gauze or a leash.
  • Lifting: Gently slide a heavy coat, cardboard sheet, or board underneath them to serve as a stretcher. Do not put pressure on their chest or abdomen.
  • Bleeding Control: Apply clean direct pressure to active wounds with sterile gauze or clean towels. Do not apply tight tourniquets.
  • Immediate Vet: Keep them warm and secure in a quiet box, and drive directly to an open animal clinic.
Newborns

2. Caring for Weak, Orphaned Newborns

Newborn puppies or kittens found without their mother are extremely fragile and can pass away within hours if their body heat drops.

  • Do Not Bathe: Weak newborns cannot regulate their temperature. Wipe them down gently with a dry paper towel if needed.
  • Warmth (Highest Priority): Put them in a cardboard box lined with fleece and a heating pad set to low, wrapped in towels. Make sure they have room to crawl away if it gets too hot.
  • Do Not Feed Milk: Never feed standard milk or solid food. Feed warm kitten or puppy milk replacer via a syringe or small pet bottle, drop by drop.
  • Stimulation: Wipe their rear area with a warm damp cotton swab after each feed to stimulate bowel movements.
Dehydration

3. Treatment for Severely Dehydrated Strays

Strays wandering for days on streets suffer from chronic dehydration, which causes kidney failure and lethargy.

  • Identify Dehydration: Pull up the skin over their shoulders (the scruff). If the skin snaps back instantly, they are hydrated. If the skin stays tented or returns very slowly, they are severely dehydrated.
  • Hydration Mix: Offer plain cool water first. If they reject it, offer unflavored electrolyte water (like Pedialyte) or low-sodium chicken broth.
  • Lip Rubbing: If too weak to drink, use a dropper to place drops on their tongue and gums. Do not force large amounts down their throat.
  • Vet Fluids: Severe dehydration usually requires subcutaneous or intravenous fluids administered at a clinic.
Street Safety

4. Street Rescue Safety Protocol for Rescuers

Your safety is paramount. You cannot save the animal if you get severely injured yourself.

  • Road Traffic Safety: Never run into heavy traffic to chase an animal. Use a vehicle or warning hazard lights to secure the area if possible.
  • Rabies Awareness: Avoid animals showing heavy salivation, aggression, confusion, or lack of fear. Call local animal control authorities instead.
  • Bite Wounds: If bitten or scratched by a street animal, immediately wash the wound with soap and water for 15 minutes, and seek immediate medical attention for rabies vaccines.

Rescuer's Emergency Kit

Keep these items ready in your car or home to be prepared for stray rescue operations:

  • Heavy fleece blanket or thick towels
  • A sturdy cardboard box or carrier
  • Slip lead / dog leash & harness
  • Sterile gauze & elastic bandages
  • Electrolyte powder / unflavored Pedialyte
  • Canned soft dog & cat food (highly aromatic)
  • Clean disposable gloves & hand sanitizer
